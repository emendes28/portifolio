Lion

Lions are the only cats that live in groups, which are called prides.

  • Scientific Name : Panthera leo
  • Average Length: Male: 1.2 m (Adult, up to the shoulder), Female: 1.1 m (Adult, up to the shoulder)
  • Average Lifespan: 10– 14 years (Adult, in nature)
  • Habitat: Savannah

Lions usually hunt at night. Their prey includes antelopes, buffaloes, zebras, young elephants, rhinos, hippos, wild hogs, crocodiles and giraffes. But they also sometimes eat smaller prey like mice, birds, hares, lizards, and tortoises. They are not above stealing kills from other carnivores, like hyenas, wild dogs, cheetahs, and leopards, or scavenging spoiled meat. After a successful hunt, all the lions in the pride share the meal. But there is a pecking order, with the adult males taking first claim, followed by the lionesses, and finally, the cubs.
